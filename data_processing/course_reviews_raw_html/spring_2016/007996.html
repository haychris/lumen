--This course was organized a lot better this semester with Samory Kpotufe as the professor. Lectures are clearer and are more useful. However, it's not really necessary to go to lectures. The textbook covers the same material, and I just read the textbook instead of going to class. Samory also posts all of his lecture notes online, so there's actually no cost to skipping lecture. The problem sets don't correspond very well with the material learned in class on in the book, so they usually took more time than necessary. But that was kinda expected with ORF 245. The midterm is extremely easy, and the final is hard af. Be careful; make sure you understand exactly how to do every problem in the problem sets before the final. If you're taking this for a requirement, you have to anyways. If you're taking it for fun or as a substitute for another stats class (like ECO 202, etc), I guess I would recommend taking it. You do learn all the fundamental concepts of statistics, and they will definitely be useful. Just be ready to put in 5-10 hours each week on homework.
