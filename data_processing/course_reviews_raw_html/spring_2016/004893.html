This course was very intellectually stimulating in the beginning of the semester but overall declined as the semester went on, in correlation with my motivation in the class. At least I know that I wouldn't be happy being a metaphysician/epistemologist. The questions raised by the course are certainly interesting, Rosen's lectures are great (he explains texts and ideas perhaps even a bit /too/ well), and he chose a wide variety of texts. However, I think as the semester progressed, I became increasingly disillusioned with myself for worrying about, say, the continuity of personal identity, or how best to construct an argument attempting to refute the brain-in-the-vat hypothesis, when millions of children are dying because they don't have access to clean drinking water or their families can't put enough food on the table or climate change/severe environmental conditions have afflicted their homes. Practical Ethics was a better course for me than PHI 203.
